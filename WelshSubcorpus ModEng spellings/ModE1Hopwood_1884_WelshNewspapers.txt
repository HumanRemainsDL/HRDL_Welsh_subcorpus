3. If the deceased's bodies were burned, this would give criminals a further opportunity to escape without punishment. A few years ago a woman from Durham poisoned various relatives. After she had poisoned the seventh, she was found out. The Home Office obtained permission to open the graves. The bodies were inspected, and some poison was found on each of them. The wicked viper was put on trial; and after the jury had found her guilty, she was sentenced to the gallows. If the bodies had, in this case, been burned, it would have been impossible to find out the severity of her guilt, and the magnitude of the devilishness, that insensitive scoundrel! This reason, if we had not another reason, is enough to completely reject the custom of cremating deadly remains of unincorporated spirits, but we have others.
